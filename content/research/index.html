Water agitation in a closed tank

The overInterDyMFoam solver and Chimera (overset) grids were used for OpenFOAM.

Water agitation in a closed tank

The overInterDyMFoam solver was used in OpenFOAM.

The Chimera (overset) grids technique was use to handle the dynamic mesh. The MULES (multidimensional universal limiter with explicit solution) algorithm was used to solve the VOF (volume of fluid) Riemann equation.

In the animation, the left is under the same direction of rotation and speed of the rotators and the right is under the opposite direction of rotation but the same speed of the rotators.

Particle transport in shale fracture

The MPPICFoam solver was used for OpenFOAM.

Particle transport in shale fracture

The MPPICFoam solver was used for OpenFOAM with 3D simulation.

The simulation for 3600 seconds (1 hour) is under 8 processors done in 1 day with DPM (discrete particle model) under MP-PIC (multiphase particle in cell) techneque.

In the animation, the sand particles with everage 300µm normal distribution (100µm - 500µm) inject into the shale fold fracture with 584.8ft long, 98.4ft height and 0.3in width. Proppant density is 1000kg/m3 and sand particle density is 2650kg/m3. The flow rate is 40bpm with sand concentration 2.5lb/gal.

Hurricane simulation

The solver is developed by coupling WRF and ROMS through CESM coupler.

Hurricane simulation and forecasting

The solver was developed under MPI parallel computing coupled WRF and ROMS throough NCAR-CESM coupler.

The coupler was employed to couple atmosphere, land and ocean models based on MCT (Modeling Coupling Toolkit) for dealing with couplig of multiple grids, multiple physics and multiple processors.

In the animation, the hurricane Katrina, which emerged and developed into the Gulf of Mexico from August 27, 2005 to August 30, 2005, was simulated. The life track was fully numerically reproduced. The storm growing from a Category 3 hurricane to a Category 5 hurricane was also captured by the simulation after entering the Gulf of Mexico.
